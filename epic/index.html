Persona, user story, interaction flow & conceptual model on Valves, Steam: https://store.steampowered.com/

Persona:

*Lucy Sterling, *College undergraduate, *Age 24, *Lives in LA, California *White/Hispanic *Studies engineering at UCLA *Speaks spanish & english fluently *Relevant Technology: Custom built PC her & her brother built together

Use Case & Interaction Flow:

Recently affected by the pandemic, Lucy's friend group started getting into virtual board games and have now suggested she get it too and join them on game nights. As a new user, she wants to purchase the card game UNO so she can play with all her acquaintances and friends. To do this, once she's activated her account, installed the client and typed 'UNO' into the search bar and hits ENTER; *Steam takes her to a store page within the site, displaying a long list of relevant results* she'll need to click on the particular result she's sea rching for. Her friend Tyler said it would be the one on the very top of the page. She clicks on this result. *Steam opens up the purchase page of that particular game, displaying trailers, user feedback & pricing Once Ms. Sterling decides this is the product she's looking for, she clicks the "Add to Cart" button *Steam proceeds to take her to checkout section After she enters her card information and hits the "Confirm Purchase" selector. Vuala! *Steam displays the message "Congratulations! This game is now in your steam library and you can install it anytime* After successful use of this service, Lucy uses this site frequently and with ease. Around 4 times per week.
